About

My projects have been in many diverse areas such as Computer Graphics, Computer Vision, Finance, Simulation, Robotics, Machine Learning, Real-Time System Design, Psychology, Biokinesiology, Microbiology, Philosophy, Number Theory, among others. Here's a link to my "techie" resume: ____ I hold a Summa cum Laude BS in Mechatronics Engineering and a Masters in Biomedical Engineering. I worked as a researcher at USC during my PhD in Neuroscience where I focused on designing, simulating and implementing a variety of algorithms on robotic systems as well as cadavers for research in Neuromuscular Biomechanics. I later worked at Vidrio Technologies LLC creating laser microscopy and image analysis software for Neuroscience. I also worked at Fitch Solutions where I delivered high-stake, financial features to our clients using a broad variety of technologies. I currently work as a Researcher at The Veidt Initiative, where I focus on Optimized Human Learning, Biomedical Engineering Applications and Computational Neuroscience. My preferred weapons of choice are currently Python and C++17.

Institute for Robotics and Intelligent Systems, USC 2010-06-01 — 2010-08-01

Summer Research Intern

I was tasked with designing and implementing a system to recognize 3D gestures using C++ and real-time, point-cloud data from a PrimeSense 3D sensor. I was able to develop a system to recognize simple 3D hand-gestures using a simplified Dynamic Time Warping approach and later a modified Hidden Markov Model approach. Once a gesture was recognized the system sent commands to third party software applications. The project was developed using C++, OpenGL and OpenCV, in addition to Microchip assembly language for an input board.
